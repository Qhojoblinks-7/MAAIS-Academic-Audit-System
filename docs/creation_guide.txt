================================================================================
          GUIDE TO CREATING ACADEMIC STRUCTURES (YEARS, CLASSES & DEPARTMENTS)
================================================================================

This guide explains how the dynamic creation features work and how to add Year
Groups, Classrooms, and Departments within the Administrative panel.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. HOW TO ADD A YEAR GROUP (Academic Architect)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
To establish a new academic grade level (e.g., "SHS 4"):
  1. Navigate to "Subject Curriculum" (/academic-architect) in the sidebar.
  2. Locate the "Institutional Blueprint" panel on the left side of the screen.
  3. Click the "+ Add Year Group" button next to the blueprint title.
  4. In the modal that appears:
     - Enter the "Year Group Name" (e.g., "SHS 4").
     - Select/toggle the programs you want associated with this level (e.g.,
       "Science", "General Arts", "Business", "Vocational").
  5. Click "Create Level".
  6. The level will instantly spawn as a reactive node in the blueprint tree 
     and automatically expand to show its associated programs.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. HOW TO ADD A CLASSROOM UNIT (Academic Architect)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Once a Year Group and its programs exist, you can add physical classroom sections
(e.g., "1 Science 3"):
  1. Go to "Subject Curriculum" (/academic-architect) and expand your Year Group node.
  2. Under the specific Program block (e.g., "Science"), click the dotted button 
     labeled "+ Add Classroom Unit".
  3. In the modal that appears:
     - Enter the "Classroom Name" (e.g., "1 Science 3").
     - Define the maximum "Capacity Load" (default is 45 students).
     - Enter the "Initial Student Count".
  4. Click "Add Classroom".
  5. The classroom will immediately populate under that program, automatically
     allocating and distributing students to the school houses (Guggisberg,
     Aggrey, Nkrumah) and updating the Subject Curriculum Matrix table in real-time.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. HOW TO SPAWN A DEPARTMENT CLUSTER (Department Management)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
To spawn an entire academic department cluster with associated HODs:
  1. Navigate to "Department Management" in the admin dashboard.
  2. Click the "+ Spawn Department" button at the top right of the screen.
  3. In the modal that appears:
     - Enter the "Department Name" (e.g., "Vocational Department").
     - Enter the "Initial HOD Name" (e.g., "Anthony Hackman") to assign a leader.
     - Provide a qualitative description of the department's academic scope.
  4. Click "Spawn Cluster".
  5. The department is instantly created with a randomized high-fidelity color
     theme, standard compliance checklists, auto-generated teacher profiles, and
     added into the active school registries.
================================================================================
The "kebab" buttons (three-dot vertical ellipsis menus) in the class structures and year groups are contextual action points for administrative operations.
In this system's architecture, they represent administrative controls designed for the following actions:
1. Year Group Kebabs
Located on the far right of each Year Group block, these are meant for broad academic level configurations:
Level Promotion / Rollover: Programmatically rolling over students from one year group to the next (e.g., promoting SHS 1 to SHS 2).
Program Restructuring: Adding or removing major curriculum tracks (like Business or Science) assigned to that level.
Archiving/Deactivation: Archiving the level census data at the end of an academic year.
2. Classroom Unit Kebabs
Located on the right of each individual Classroom row (e.g., next to the house distribution circles), these are designed for specific section management:
Student Transfers: Opening a portal to batch-transfer students between sister classrooms.
Capacity Overrides: Adjusting the classroom's enrollment limits and student load thresholds in real time.
House Rebalancing: Initiating a re-allocation of students into Guggisberg, Aggrey, and Nkrumah houses.
Unit Dissolution: Archiving or deleting the specific class unit if it is no longer active.
